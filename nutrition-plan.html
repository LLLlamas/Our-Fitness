01.The Strategy

The problem isn't picky eating — it's appetite and pattern. You have a great list of foods. The job is to (a) eat more often than you feel like, (b) hide calories in liquids since you already love smoothies, and (c) keep cost down.

01
Drink your calories
Smoothies and chocolate milk bypass the "I'm not hungry" problem. One loaded smoothie = 700–900 cal in 4 minutes. You already make these — just upgrade them with oats, extra honey, and a banana you'd otherwise skip.
02
Eat by the clock, not hunger
Hardgainers can't trust appetite. Set 5 anchors: wake, mid-morning, post-hoops, mid-afternoon, dinner. Set phone alarms. Treat each one like a HoopCheck wake notification — non-negotiable.
03
Use what you already love
Spam, rice, eggs, chocolate milk, smoothies, pizza, chicken nuggets. These are all on your list and all calorie-dense. We're not introducing kale. We're stacking the foods you'll actually finish.
04
Post-hoops is your magic window
After your morning court session at Bliss St you'll have a 60-min window where appetite returns and protein absorbs best. Chocolate milk + a bagel + eggs in this window does more than dinner.
05
Cheap calorie multipliers
Olive oil ($0.12/tbsp = 120 cal), honey ($0.10/tbsp = 60 cal), butter ($0.10/tbsp = 100 cal), oats ($0.15/cup = 300 cal). Add one of these to anything and it gets denser without feeling bigger.
!
Track weight weekly
Same day, morning, after bathroom, before eating. Goal: +0.25 to +0.5 lb/week. If you stall 2 weeks, add 200 cal (one extra smoothie scoop + tbsp honey). If gaining too fast, drop oil.

03.Sample Week

A non-rigid template. Repeat what works, swap what doesn't. Hoops days have heavier breakfasts and post-workout shakes; rest days lean lighter on carbs but keep protein steady.

04.Grocery + Cost

Weekly shop priced at NYC averages, May 2026. Trader Joe's, C-Town, or Key Food on Queens Blvd will all hit close to these numbers. Costco for bulk items if you have access. Eggs and bagels you can hit any deli for.

Money-saving notes: Buy 18-count eggs at Costco/Trader Joe's. Frozen berries cost half of fresh and work better in smoothies. Whey protein lasts 5–6 weeks on this plan — split that cost across weeks. Spam is on sale most weeks at C-Town. Generic store-brand cereal is identical to name brand for ~40% less.
